User Rules
Material is made available during our opening hours subject to agreement with depositors, to statutory and other closure periods and with the approval of the Head Archivist.

  1. Readers are required to enter their name, address and signature in the visitors' book. Please remember by signing the book you have agreed to read and observe these regulations.
  2. For security reasons bags and papers may be opened and examined during your visit. An initial inspection will be made at the Security Point on the ground floor. If bags or their contents are considered to be unacceptable, London Metropolitan Archives reserves the right to refuse admission.
  3. Public areas are monitored by closed circuit television (CCTV) and recorded on video.
  4. Animals, except for assistance dogs, are not permitted in any area of London Metropolitan Archives.
  5. Smoking is not permitted in any area of London Metropolitan Archives.
  6. Readers may not bring briefcases, bags, suitcases and outdoor clothing into the rooms on the Information Floor. Please use the coat racks and lockers in the Visitor Lounge on the mezzanine floor.
  7. Personal stereos and portable scanners or copiers are not permitted in the rooms on the Information Floor.
  8. Mobile telephones may not be switched on or used in the Archive Study Area. Mobile phones may be switched on in the Information Area but the ring tone should be muted and conversations should take place in the Welcome Area or Visitor Lounge.
  9. Readers may not bring into the rooms on the Information Floor any item that might damage material held by London Metropolitan Archives. This includes food, drink, confectionery, glue, bottles of ink, correction fluid, scissors, knives, highlighter pens and adhesive tape.
  10. Personal computers, tape recorders and typewriters may be used at the discretion of the duty staff in designated areas and provided they do not disturb other readers. London Metropolitan Archives reserves the right to undertake safety checks of readers' machines at any time.
  11. Readers are asked to show consideration for other readers and may be requested to limit the time they spend on microform machines or the computer terminals. A machine left unattended for twenty minutes may be allocated to another reader at the discretion of the duty staff.
  12. Readers should not behave in a way that threatens or harasses members of staff or other readers.
  13. The Archive Study Area is a quiet study area. Readers are required to maintain a reasonable silence while working there. Talking in other public areas should be done quietly. Readers should not behave in a way likely to disturb others.
  14. All material is issued to readers at the discretion of staff, usually one item at a time. Issue is strictly controlled in the interests of security and conservation. Material issued in the Archive Study Area may not be removed from that room.
  15. Readers are required to handle material with care in accordance with London Metropolitan Archives Handling Guidelines. Copies of the guidelines are available in the Archive Study Area.
  16. Items that have been issued to a particular reader must not be handed to another reader without the knowledge and consent of the duty staff. Readers may not handle items that have been issued to another reader or take such items from another reader's desk without permission from the duty staff. Where more than one reader has requested the same item at the same time, the decision to allocate the item rests with staff. Staff are empowered to ask readers to relinquish an item that they are using.
  17. Before leaving the Archive Study Area readers must return all material issued to them to a member of staff at the Collection Point. Readers are held responsible for items issued to them until they have been returned to the Collection Point.
  18. Copies of material may be provided in accordance with London Metropolitan Archives copying policy. Copies are made available within limits determined by conservation needs, the conditions of deposit and the requirements of copyright legislation. Copies of the policy are available on request.
  19. Members of staff are unable to give legal advice.
  20. City of London audit rules do not permit us to open the till in order to give change. Change machines are available in the Information Area.
  21. Lost property will be retained for three months from the date it is left at the Archives. It will then be disposed of.
