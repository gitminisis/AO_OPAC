The Archives and Information Management System (AIMS) is an integrated and user-friendly online tool to search our archival, library, and art collections in one place. You can search our collections and submit an inquiry without an account.

Creating an online account allows you to track your inquiries, request records to view in our Reading Room, and bookmark your favourites.

SET UP AN ONLINE ACCOUNT

AIMS online accounts use Public Secure, which is a Government of Ontario login required to access online Government of Ontario services. Click here for more information.

Option A:

1. On the Public Secure page, log in using your email and password.

2. You will then be directed to an account registration page for AIMS.

3. Follow the prompts in AIMS to set up your account with the Archives of Ontario. You will receive a confirmation email that you have successfully registered. If you don't see the email in your inbox, check your junk or spam folder.

4. Click the link in your confirmation email to be directed to the AIMS portal to begin your search.

Option B:

If you don't already have a Public Secure account, follow these steps:

1. On the Public Secure page, click the My Ontario login button.

2. Under New User, click the Create login button. Enter your email address and create a password. The Public Secure system will send you an email asking you to verify your account.

3. Verify your account by clicking on the link in the email. You will have 30 minutes to click this link. If you don't see the email in your inbox, check your junk or spam folder.

4. Once you have verified your account, sign in to Public Secure using your My Ontario login credentials.

5. After you have activated your Public Secure account, you will be directed to an account registration page for AIMS.

6. Follow the prompts in AIMS to set up your account with the Archives of Ontario. You will receive a confirmation email that you have successfully registered. If you don't see the email in your inbox, check your junk or spam folder.

7. Click the link in your confirmation email to be directed to the AIMS portal to begin your search.

To View Records at the Archives of Ontario

An Archives of Ontario Researcher (AOR) card is required to view records in-person in our Reading Room. Click here to register online.

To obtain your AOR card upon arrival at the Archives of Ontario, you will be required to show one (1) piece of Government issued ID (such as a Driver's Licence, Passport, or a Government photo identification card). Note that a Provincial Health card will not be accepted as a valid form of ID.
