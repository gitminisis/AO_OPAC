Account Registration

The Archives of Ontario Online Public Access site is an online portal for citizens and the public at large to access Government of Ontario holdings from archival, art and library perspectives. To create a better experience, the Archives, Art and Library portal is designed to allow anyone to search free and anonymously however a better experience for users is available if one registers for an online account. This account will integrate with the generic Government of Ontario one account for all services initiative known as “One Key”. More information on One Key, can be found at the following link: https://www.one-key.gov.on.ca/iaalogin/IAALogin.jsp

Who can register for an Account?

Anyone over the age of 14 can apply for an Online account.

Why do I need one?

An online account is not required to use and access the services of the Archives. However, having an account will provide more immediate access, notifications from the Archives along with requesting of holdings, requesting reproductions and other services the Archives of Ontario provides. An online account will also enable you to carry out your research more effectively by providing access to enhanced features in this catalogue. You'll be able to request original documents to view them in the Archives Study Area, make Bookmarks (create a list of documents in the catalogue for future reference) and see a list of documents that you've previously requested during an earlier visit. You can also request documents up to two weeks in advance, reserving them for your visit and ensuring that you can make the most of your research time.

How do I get one?

You can complete the registration form using the computers at the Archives or start your registration immediately by clicking the button below. Your account will be automatically enabled to start the ability to request and track interactions with the Archives, Art and Library holdings. If you have any questions or need assistance, please contact the Archives of Ontario and one of the service staff are available to assist via telephone during the normal hours of operations at: 1-800-668-9933.

To request (in person) to review records of the Archives, Art or Library collections, researchers will still require two proof of identity documents (one proof of identity and one proof of address) to the Service Desk. Once your identification is validated your online account will ease requests in the future.

What documents will I need to register?

Acceptable documents for registration are:

Proof of your identity (must include a valid signature):

  • Passport
  • Driving Licence
  • National ID card
  • Police/Customs/Home Office/warrant card
  • Canadian Forces ID card
  • Provincial ID card
  • Provincial Government ID

Proof of your address (internet printouts or online statements are not accepted):

  • Utility bill (issued within the last three months)
  • Bank/building society statement (issued within the last three months)
  • Credit card statement (issued within the last three months)
  • Passports with address (where the address has been officially entered by the issuing authority)
  • National ID card with address
  • Driving licence with address
  • University certificate for hall of residence (stamped and signed by institution)
  • Firearms licence
  • Home Insurance policy
  • International student photocard

Proof for students

If you are aged 14 or over and still at school or college you should bring a letter on headed notepaper, signed by your teacher or tutor, together with some evidence of your age, such as a passport or birth certificate.
